About Me


I am a data scientist working at the intersection of medicine and machine learning, aiming to bridge the gap between these two worlds. My passion is developing computational tools to learn useful information from vast troves of biomedical data (images, text, genomics, etc.) and finding ways to apply them in the realms of clinical medicine and basic research in order to tangibly improve patient care and advance our fundamental knowledge of the biological processes underlying disease. I am interested in both the technical aspects (data engineering, software development, machine learning methods, etc.) as well as the biomedical aspects (clinical workflows, diagnostics, cancer biology, etc.) of translational machine learning.

I currently work as a data scientist on the AI Operations group at Dana-Farber Cancer Institute in Boston. Before that, I studied data science at Harvard University. Before that, I studied biology at Oberlin College. Before that, I grew up in Seattle.

Hobbies include running, hiking, exploring new breweries with friends, taking care of my houseplants, and drinking copious amounts of coffee.

Research

I am interested in the application of machine learning and other computational approaches to learn from high-dimensional biological/medical data including imaging, *-omics, and text. How can we use these data to improve patient care and gain a better understanding of the underlying biological processes causing disease?
